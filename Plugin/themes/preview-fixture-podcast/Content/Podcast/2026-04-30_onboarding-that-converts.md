The best onboarding is the one the user does not notice. This week we trace a
single app's first-run experience from five screens down to two – and what the
analytics did next.

## Find the first real moment of value

Write down the exact instant a new user gets something they came for. Every
screen *before* that instant is a tax. Some taxes are worth paying; most are
not. Once you can name the moment, the cuts become obvious.

## Ask for permissions late

Notifications, contacts, location – none of them belong on screen one. Ask the
moment the permission unlocks something the user already wants, and the grant
rate roughly doubles. Ask up front and you have traded a yes for a maybe.
