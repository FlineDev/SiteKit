A hard deadline does not make you faster. It makes you **honest** – about which
features were ever load-bearing and which were there because they were fun to
build. This week we walk through the last sprint before a release and the three
calls that mattered.

## Scope is the only lever you control

You cannot add people late, you cannot add hours forever, and you cannot lower
quality without paying it back with interest. Scope is the one dial that moves
freely – so the question is never *"how do we go faster"* but *"what is the
smallest thing worth shipping"*.

## The cuts that did not hurt

We pulled the iPad layout, the share extension, and the onboarding video. Two
weeks after launch, not a single support email asked for any of them. The
features you are most nervous to cut are often the ones nobody was waiting for.

> The deadline did not lower our standards. It raised them – by forcing every
> remaining feature to justify the space it took.
