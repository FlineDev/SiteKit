# Swedish (sv) – Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` first. Swedish-specific rules for SiteKit content. Apple's Xcode 26+ Swedish guide is the authoritative app-string complement.

## Address & formality
- Swedish is **informal by default** – "du" is used for everyone, including business and institutional sites (the "du-reform"). Capitalized "Ni" is rare and can read as old-fashioned; avoid unless deliberately formal. Keep tone direct and friendly.

## Quotation marks
- Use ” … ” (right-high on both sides) or « … »; ” … ” is most common online. Be consistent.

## Punctuation & spacing
- No space before `: ; ! ?`. The letters `å ä ö` are distinct letters, not accented a/o – never substitute `a`/`o`.

## Numbers & dates
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **non-breaking space**: `1 234,50 kr` (currency after). Don't reformat numbers in placeholders.
- Dates: ISO-style `2026-06-14` is very common and idiomatic; or `14 juni 2026` – **months lowercase**.

## Capitalization & terminology
- **Sentence case** for headings; do not capitalize months, weekdays, languages, or nationalities (`svenska`, `måndag`, `juni`).
- Common web terms: `inlägg`/`artikel` (post), `prenumerera` (subscribe), kept `nyhetsbrev`/`newsletter`, `integritet` (privacy).

## Common pitfalls
- Compounds are written **closed** (`användarnamn`, `integritetspolicy`); splitting them ("särskrivning") changes meaning and is a classic error.
- False friends: "aktuell" = *current/relevant*; "eventuell" = *possible* (not *eventual*).
