# Norwegian Bokmål (nb) – Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` first. Bokmål rules for SiteKit content. Apple's Xcode 26+ Norwegian guide is the authoritative app-string complement.

## Address & formality
- Norwegian is **informal by default** – "du" for everyone, including business sites. Formal "De" is archaic; avoid it. Keep tone direct and plain.

## Quotation marks
- Use « … » (guillemets, no inner space) or ” … ”; guillemets are traditional in print, ” … ” common online. Be consistent.

## Punctuation & spacing
- No space before `: ; ! ?`. The letters `æ ø å` are distinct – never substitute `ae`/`o`/`a`.

## Numbers & dates
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **non-breaking space**: `1 234,50 kr` (currency after). Don't reformat numbers in placeholders.
- Dates: `14. juni 2026` (note the period after the day) – **months lowercase**.

## Capitalization & terminology
- **Sentence case** for headings; do not capitalize months, weekdays, languages, or nationalities (`norsk`, `mandag`, `juni`).
- Common web terms: `innlegg`/`artikkel` (post), `abonner` (subscribe), kept `nyhetsbrev`/`newsletter`, `personvern` (privacy).

## Common pitfalls
- Compounds are **closed** (`brukernavn`, `personvernerklæring`); splitting them is a frequent, meaning-changing error.
- Don't confuse Bokmål with Nynorsk – pick Bokmål (`nb`) unless the site explicitly wants Nynorsk (`nn`).
- False friends: "aktuell" = *current/relevant*; "eventuell" = *possible*.
