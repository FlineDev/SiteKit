# Ukrainian (uk) вАУ Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` first. Ukrainian-specific rules for SiteKit content (Cyrillic script).

## Address & formality
- Formal **"–≤–Є" (vy)** is the default polite address for a public site; informal **"—В–Є" (ty)** suits a casual, personal one. Pick one and keep it.
- For directly addressing a named person, Ukrainian uses the **vocative case** (`–Ю–ї–µ–Ї—Б–∞–љ–і—А–µ`, `–њ–∞–љ—Ц –Ю–ї–µ–љ–Њ`) вАУ not the nominative.

## Quotation marks
- Use ¬Ђ вА¶ ¬ї (lapky) for primary quotes; nested quotes use вАЮ вА¶ вАЬ.

## Numbers & dates
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **non-breaking space**: `1 234,50 вВі` (currency after). Don't reformat numbers inside placeholders.
- Dates: `14 —З–µ—А–≤–љ—П 2026` (the month takes the genitive form `—З–µ—А–≤–љ—П`) or `14.06.2026` вАУ **months lowercase**.

## Capitalization & terminology
- **Sentence case** for headings; do not capitalize months, weekdays, languages, or nationalities (`—Г–Ї—А–∞—Ч–љ—Б—М–Ї–∞`, `–њ–Њ–љ–µ–і—Ц–ї–Њ–Ї`, `—З–µ—А–≤–µ–љ—М`).
- Common web terms: `–і–Њ–њ–Є—Б`/`—Б—В–∞—В—В—П` (post), `–њ—Ц–і–њ–Є—Б–∞—В–Є—Б—П` (subscribe), kept `—А–Њ–Ј—Б–Є–ї–Ї–∞`/`newsletter`, `–Ї–Њ–љ—Д—Ц–і–µ–љ—Ж—Ц–є–љ—Ц—Б—В—М` (privacy), `–Ј–∞—Б—В–Њ—Б—Г–љ–Њ–Ї` (app).

## Common pitfalls
- **Write Ukrainian, not russified Ukrainian.** Avoid surzhyk and Russian calques вАУ use established Ukrainian terms and spelling. This matters to readers.
- **Gender agreement:** past-tense verbs and adjectives inflect for gender. When the reader's gender is unknown, prefer neutral phrasing, plural, or infinitive constructions rather than defaulting to masculine.
- The letters `—Ц —Ч —Ф “С` are distinct Ukrainian letters вАУ never substitute Russian `–Є —Л —Н –≥`.
