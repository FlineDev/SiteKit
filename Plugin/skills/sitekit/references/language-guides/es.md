# Spanish (es) – Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` first. Spanish-specific rules for SiteKit content. If the site targets Latin America vs. Spain, note the regional differences below.

## Address & formality
- **"tú"** is friendly and common for blogs, products, and most modern sites. **"usted"** is more formal/institutional and standard in parts of Latin America for addressing customers. **"vosotros"** (informal plural) is Spain-only; Latin America uses **"ustedes"** for all plural. Pick a register and a region target, then stay consistent.

## Quotation marks
- Spain traditionally uses angular quotes « … »; “ … ” (curly) is widely accepted and common online. Pick one and keep it. Nested quotes switch to the other style.

## Punctuation & spacing
- **Opening question and exclamation marks are required**: `¿Cómo estás?`, `¡Bienvenido!`. Omitting them is the clearest machine-translation tell.
- No space before `:` `;` `?` `!`.

## Numbers & dates
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **point** in Spain (`1.234,50 €`); many Latin American countries use the **point** for decimals (`1,234.50`). Match the target region. Currency symbol usually after the amount with a space in Spain.
- Dates: `14 de junio de 2026` – **months lowercase**, day first.

## Capitalization & terminology
- **Sentence case** for headings; do not capitalize months, weekdays, or languages (`español`, `lunes`, `junio`).
- Common web terms: `entrada`/`artículo` (post), `suscribirse` (subscribe), `boletín` or kept `newsletter`, `privacidad` (privacy), `aviso legal` (legal notice).

## Common pitfalls
- False friends: "actual" = *current* (not *actual* → `real`); "actualmente" = *currently*. "asistir" = *to attend* (not *assist* → `ayudar`).
- Use inverted opening punctuation even mid-sentence: `Vale, ¿lo intentamos?`
- Inclusive language: prefer neutral collective nouns (`el equipo`, `la comunidad`); the `-e`/`@`/`x` endings are debated – follow the site owner's preference, default to neutral phrasing.
