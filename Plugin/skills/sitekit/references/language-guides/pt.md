# Portuguese (pt) – Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` first. Covers European (pt-PT) and Brazilian (pt-BR) Portuguese; choose a target and stay consistent.

## Address & formality
- **Brazil (pt-BR):** "você" is the standard friendly address for most sites. **Portugal (pt-PT):** "você" can sound distant; many sites use the implied second person or "tu" for casual, formal address otherwise. Pick a regional target with the site owner.
- The two variants differ in vocabulary, spelling, and some grammar – do not mix them on one site.

## Quotation marks
- pt-PT traditionally uses « … »; pt-BR uses “ … ”. Match the variant.

## Punctuation & spacing
- No space before `: ; ! ?`. Use correct diacritics: `ã õ` (nasal), `ç`, `á é í ó ú`, `â ê ô` – they change meaning.

## Numbers & dates
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **point**: `1.234,50`. Currency: `€ 1.234,50` (pt-PT) / `R$ 1.234,50` (pt-BR). Don't reformat numbers in placeholders.
- Dates: `14 de junho de 2026` – **months lowercase**.

## Capitalization & terminology
- **Sentence case** for headings; months, weekdays, and languages lowercase (`português`, `segunda-feira`, `junho`).
- Common web terms: `publicação`/`post` (post), `inscrever-se`/`assinar` (subscribe; pt-BR favours `assinar` for newsletters), kept `newsletter`, `privacidade` (privacy).

## Common pitfalls
- False friends: "atual" = *current*; "eventualmente" = *occasionally/possibly* (not *eventually*); "pretender" = *to intend*.
- pt-BR vs pt-PT vocabulary: `arquivo` vs `ficheiro` (file), `tela` vs `ecrã` (screen), `usuário` vs `utilizador` (user). Use the target variant's term.
