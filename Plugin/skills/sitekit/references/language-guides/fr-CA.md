# Canadian French (fr-CA) – Web Content Style Guide

Read `principles.md` and `fr.md` first – the France-French rules mostly hold. This file lists the Québec/Canada differences.

## Vocabulary – avoid anglicisms
- Québec French actively replaces English loanwords that France often keeps: `courriel` (not e-mail), `clavardage` (chat), `magasiner` (to shop), `téléverser` (upload), `baladodiffusion` / `balado` (podcast). Prefer the French term.
- A few everyday words shift meaning: `déjeuner` = breakfast, `dîner` = lunch, `souper` = dinner.

## Address & formality
- **"vous"** is the standard public-site address, as in France.

## Punctuation & spacing
- Keep the non-breaking space **before `:`**. The thin space before `; ! ?` is recommended but applied less consistently online than in France – be consistent within the site.

## Numbers, dates & currency
- Decimal **comma**, thousands **non-breaking space**: `1 234,50`. The dollar sign goes **after** the amount with a space: `12,50 $`. Don't reformat numbers inside placeholders.
- Dates: `14 juin 2026` – **months lowercase**.

## Common pitfalls
- Don't ship France-specific vocabulary (e-mail, week-end, shopping) to a Québec audience – use the French equivalents above.
- Capitalization, quotation marks (« … » with inner spaces), and sentence-case headings follow `fr.md`.
